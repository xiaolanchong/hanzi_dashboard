---- 羹 [gēng] thick soup; 调羹 tiáogēng spoon  [láng]
羹 ⁴gēng {E} n. thick soup M:wǎn 碗
From 羔 (gāo) 'lamb' over 美 (měi) 'beautiful'.
"If the character for beautiful is put together with the one for lamb, it makes the name for a meat soup or stew called gēng, described in many texts as early as 500 B.C." --Lindqvist.
