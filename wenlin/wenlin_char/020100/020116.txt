160  五 [wǔ] five; 五月 Wǔyuè May
五 ¹wǔ* {A} num. five; 5 ◆n. 〈mus.〉 no. 6 in gōngchěpǔ
"五行也。从二, 陰陽在天地間。" --說文。
"The two principles yīn and yáng, begetting the five elements, between heaven and earth." --Wieger.
Karlgren regards this yīnyáng explanation as "far-fetched". Modern scholars have other theories which seem no more enlightening.
