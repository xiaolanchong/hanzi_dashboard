123  公 [gōng] public; 公里 kilometer; 办公室 office; 公司 company
公 ²gōng {C} b.f. ①public affairs; official duties 公事 ³gōngshì ②(following a surname) respectful form of address for an elderly man ③public; state-owned; collective 公立 ¹gōnglì ④common; accepted 公认 gōngrèn ⑤metric 公尺 gōngchǐ ⑥just; fair; impartial 公正 ¹gōngzhèng* ⑦male (of animals) 公鸡 ²gōngjī ⑧make public 公开 gōngkāi* ◆n. a gongchepu note
"The scholastic interpretation is: the right 八 division of 厶 private things" --Karlgren(1923) (厶 is short for 私 sī 'private').
Compare 共 gòng ‘share’.
公 in ancient Chinese texts is commonly translated as ‘duke’ (Legge) or ‘feudal lord’; cp. 大公, 公爵, 王公, 公國, 公侯, 周公; Karlgen has 公 ‘father; prince’; cp. 侯 ‘marquis’ (Karlgen ‘feudatory prince; princely’)
