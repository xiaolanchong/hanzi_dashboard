883  幸 [xìng] good fortune, luck; 幸福 xìngfú happy  [niè]
幸/倖 ⁴xìng {E} b.f. ①luck; good fortune 幸运 xìngyùn* 幸福 xìngfú* ②fortunately; luckily 幸亏 xìngkuī ③〈wr.〉 favor 得幸 ²déxìng ④〈wr.〉 come; arrive (of an emperor) 巡幸 xúnxìng* ⑤〈wr.〉 hope; trust 幸勿推却 xìngwùtuīquè ◆n. Surname
The explanation is uncertain. There were two words, 幸 xìng 'luck' and 幸 niè 'criminal'; the latter is obsolete. Karlgren(1940) said 幸 niè 'criminal' depicted manacles; ("Ancient handcuffs" says 王宏源). 幸 xìng 'luck' may or may not have been related.
In 執(执) zhí, 報(报) bào etc., 幸 has the meaning 'criminal'.
Not to be confused with 辛 xīn 'suffering'.
