---- 袂 [mèi] sleeve of a robe; 联袂 liánmèi get married; side by side  [yì]
袂 ⁶mèi b.f. 〈wr.〉 sleeve 把袂 bǎmèi
From 衤(衣 yī) 'clothing' and 夬 guài (formerly phonetic).
"The sleeve of a robe" --Mathews.
According to Karlgren(1923), 袂 was used for a different word, also meaning 'sleeve', that was pronounced similarly to 夬 kuài. That word is now obsolete, and the character 袂 has been applied to the synonym mèi 'sleeve'.
