正 ⁶zhēng {E} b.f. first month of the lunar year 正月 zhēngyuè* 新正 xīnzhēng
From 止 (zhǐ 'stop'), picture of a foot, with something at the top, over the toes: once a box, then a circle, and finally a straight line.
"To 止 stop at the 一 line ...without going astray" --Wieger.
The top may originally have been an old form of 丁 dīng, phonetic, according to Karlgren(1940).
In 是 shì and 定 dìng, 正 is written 𤴓. 乏 fá was originally 正 backwards. 各 gè was 正 upside-down.
