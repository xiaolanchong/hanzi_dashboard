1902 秦 [Qín] the Qin (Ch'in) Dynasty, 221-207 B.C. (Qín --> China)
秦 ¹Qín {E} n. ①important early dynasty (221-206 B.C.) ②short name for Shaanxi province
From 𡗗 'hands holding pestle' over 禾 (hé) 'grain'.
Originally a kind of rice, then the name of the valley where this rice grew and the short-lived but infamous Qín state originated.
"Below, 禾; above, the seal has 廾 () two hands holding 午 a pestle, now badly contracted" --Karlgren(1923). In the earliest forms, 禾 was doubled.
