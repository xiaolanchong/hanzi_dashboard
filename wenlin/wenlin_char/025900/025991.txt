115  文 [wén] language, literature; 文化 wénhuà culture
文 ¹wén {D} b.f. ①writing; script 文字 wénzì* ②language 语文 yǔwén* ③literary/artistic/cultural pursuits 文艺 ¹wényì* ④literary language 文言 wényán* ⑤formal ritual 虚文 xūwén ⑥certain natural phenomena 天文 tiānwén ⑦civil; civilian 文官 wénguān* ⑧cultured; cultural; literary 文化 ¹wénhuà* ⑨gentle; refined 文雅 wényǎ* ◆s.v. literary, bookish (of language) ◆n. ①〈lg.〉 inscription ②Surname ◆m. 〈trad.〉 (for copper coins) ◆v. ①cover up; conceal ②〈trad.〉 tattoo
纹/文[紋/-] ³wén {E} n. ①vein; grain ②lines/patterns (in cloth/etc.)
文 originally depicted a person with some kind of design, possibly a tattoo, on the upper body (Karlgren(1940), Lindqvist, 李乐毅, and 王宏源 all support the tattoo theory). 花纹 huāwén means 'decorative pattern', or 'tattoo'; 文身 wénshēn is a literary word for 'tattoo'.
文 wén formerly meant 'lines', 'veins', 'wood-grain'. That meaning is now written 纹(紋) wén, which is 文 plus 纟(糸 sī) 'silk'. The extended meanings: 'ornaments, designs, written characters, writing, literature, language, culture, civilization, civil (as contrasted with military), gentle, etc.' illustrate the traditional high respect for literacy in China.
文 is the 67th of the 214 radicals.
