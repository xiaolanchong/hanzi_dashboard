1804 尸 [shī] (radical 44); (F屍) 尸体 shītǐ dead body, corpse
尸[屍] ⁷shī {E} b.f. corpse 尸体 ¹shītǐ 尸首 shīshou ◆n. person representing the spirit of the dead person during sacrifices (usu. a child)
"Corpse, body; sit as representative of the dead at sacrifices; superintend --- picture of a sitting or lying man" --Karlgren(1923).
"A seated man. The living person who anciently represented the dead; by extension, a dead person." --Wieger.
The full form 屍 has 死 (sǐ) 'death' added to it (but the radical is always 尸).
