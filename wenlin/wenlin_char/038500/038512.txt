1171 陰(S阴) [yīn] opposite of 陽 yáng; 陰沉, 陰天 cloudy; 陰暗 gloomy  [yìn] [ān]
陰[阴] ³yīn {A} n. ①〈phil.〉 female/passive/negative principle in nature ②Surname ◆b.f. ①the moon 阴历 ¹yīnlì ②shaded orientation 背阴 bèiyīn(r)* ③covert; concealed; hidden 阴沟 yīngōu* ④vagina 阴道 ¹yīndào ⑤penis 阴茎 yīnjīng ⑥of the netherworld 阴间 yīnjiān* ⑦negative 阴极 ¹yīnjí ⑧north side of a hill ⑨south bank of a river ⑩reverse side of a stele 碑阴 bēiyīn ⑪in intaglio 阴文 ¹yīnwén ◆s.v. ①overcast | Tiān ∼ le. 天∼了。 It's getting overcast. ②sinister; treacherous | Nàge rén hěn ∼. 那个人很∼。 That person is very sinister.
(Explanation from the entry for the simple form 阴:) Originally 侌, from 今 (jīn) 'now' over 云 (yún) 'cloud':
"今 presence of 云 clouds" --Karlgren.
阝(阜 fù) 'hill' was added to describe the dark, shady side of a hill, and in the simple form, 月 (yuè) 'moon' replaces 侌.
A literary name for the Moon is 太阴 Tàiyīn, which goes with 太阳 Tàiyáng, the Sun.
