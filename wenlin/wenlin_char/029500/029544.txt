661  獨(S独) [dú] only, alone
獨[独] ³dú {D} adv. alone; in solitude ◆b.f. ①only; singly 单独 ¹dāndú ②old people without offspring 鳏寡孤独 guān-guǎ-gū-dú
(Explanation from the entry for the simple form 独:) From 犭(犬 quǎn) 'dog' and 蜀 shǔ ('silkworm') phonetic, simplified to 虫 (chóng 'worm').
Why is 犭(犬 quǎn) 'dog' a component in 独 'alone'? Shuōwén says: "羊爲群, 犬爲獨 Yáng wéi qún, quǎn wéi dú." Maybe this refers to a lone sheep dog guarding a 群 qún herd of sheep.
